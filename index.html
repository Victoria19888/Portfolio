My journey into computer science started in junior year of high school. I was curious about hackers and digital forensics, and by my third year, I already knew the different types of hackers from watching countless YouTube videos and taking computer science classes.

What really drew me in wasn’t just cybersecurity itself, but the investigative side of it. I’ve always loved solving problems and figuring out how things work behind the scenes. Watching crime and forensic shows made me realize how much I enjoyed tracing clues, understanding motives, and uncovering how things connect. That same mindset easily translated into cybersecurity; where every vulnerability, exploit, or network issue is like a digital mystery waiting to be solved.

Now I’m studying Computer Science (Honors) at the University of Windsor, specializing in Networks and Security. I’ve worked on projects that let me explore Linux, network analysis, and automation using tools like Wireshark and Nmap. My goal is to become a purple team hacker, combining both offensive and defensive skills to understand threats and build stronger protections...
